# LeoNes — Brand Strategy

## The name, decoded
LeoNes reads two ways at once, on purpose:
- **"Leo Nes"** — playful, modern, brandable, works in English and Spanish/Portuguese markets.
- **"Leonas"** — Spanish for lionesses. Brave. Protective. Warm with their own. Never mean.

That double meaning is the whole brand strategy in one word. Every other beauty brand for this age group picks *either* soft-and-sweet (Glossier-for-kids, all pastels, no spine) *or* loud-and-sassy (mall brands, glitter and attitude, no warmth). LeoNes is the first to claim **soft AND brave**. A lip gloss that makes you feel like a little lioness — kind to her friends, confident enough to roar.

This is our wedge. Not "cute cosmetics for kids." **Confidence, wrapped in magic.**

## Positioning statement
*For girls 8–13 who are discovering who they are, LeoNes is the cosmetic brand that turns getting ready into a magical ritual of self-belief — because unlike toy-store makeup or repackaged adult brands, every LeoNes product is designed, tested, and dosed for a girl's real skin, with one genuine moment of magic built in, in packaging she'll want to film.*

## Who we're really selling to
Two buyers, one experience:
- **The girl (8–13):** wants to feel grown-up without being rushed into "grown-up." Wants something that's *hers*. Wants it to look good on camera. Wants a story, not just a product — she'll tell her friends about "the gloss that turns pink" before she tells them what it smells like.
- **The parent (mostly moms, 30s–40s):** wants to say yes without guilt. Needs to see "dermatologist-tested," "safe," "non-toxic" *before* she sees the sparkle. She is buying permission as much as product.

Design every asset for the girl. Put the safety proof exactly where the parent's eyes will land (product page, back of pack) without ever letting it dilute the magic on the front.

## Competitive gap we're exploiting
| Brand | What they nailed | What they can't touch |
|---|---|---|
| Rhode | Minimalist premium object-design | Zero magic, zero warmth, 25-year-old customer |
| Glossier | Editorial, community-first content | Adult skincare seriousness |
| Rare Beauty | Confidence-as-message | Selena-led, not a *world* a kid can live in |
| Summer Fridays | Aspirational "I use what she uses" | Nothing kid-safe, nothing playful |
| Disney | A world, characters, total emotional buy-in | Zero premium-object design credibility; feels licensed, not owned |

**LeoNes = Rhode's object-design discipline + Disney's world-building + Rare Beauty's confidence message, dosed and priced for an 8–13-year-old and her mom.** Nobody else is standing here.

## Brand pillars (in priority order when trade-offs happen)
1. **Safety & trust** — non-negotiable, always wins a trade-off against everything else.
2. **One real moment of magic per product** — no magic, no launch.
3. **Camera-first design** — if it doesn't look good in a 9:16 frame, redesign it.
4. **Confidence over vanity** — copy and imagery always affirm who she *is*, never what she's "fixing."
5. **Collectibility** — packaging and system design should make a girl want the *next* one, not just *this* one.

## Brand architecture
- **LeoNes** — masterbrand, always the hero.
- **Prides** — product lines are called "Prides" (a group of lionesses = friend group). e.g. *The Sparkle Pride* (lip), *The Glow Pride* (skin), *The Confetti Pride* (seasonal/limited).
- **Cubs Club** — the loyalty/collector program. Points = "roars." Unlocks charms, early drops, birthday gifts.
- Every SKU ships inside a Pride, every Pride has a signature color story and one shared magical mechanic (see product doc).

## What we are never
Never cheap. Never adult. Never sexualized. Never preachy. Never a "kids' version" of a grown-up product — LeoNes is designed for this girl, not shrunk down for her.
