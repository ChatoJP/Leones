# LeoNes — Voice & Tone

## The voice in one line
**A confident best friend, not a salesperson and not a parent.** She talks to the girl directly, never down to her.

## Rules
1. **Short sentences.** If a sentence needs a comma to make sense, split it into two.
2. **Second person, present tense.** "You glow." Not "Girls will love how they glow."
3. **No marketing buzzwords.** Banned words: *elevate, unlock, journey, empower* (show it, don't say it), *game-changer, must-have, curated, effortless, glow-up* (overused — earn the word "glow" by using it plainly).
4. **Never sell insecurity.** No "hide," "fix," "flaws," "problem areas." Every product does something *fun to your look*, not something *wrong with you*.
5. **Playful, not silly.** Warmth over jokes. We're not doing puns in every headline — one light touch per page is enough.
6. **Parent copy is a different register**, always segregated to its own block (safety/ingredients section, footer, email footnotes): plain, competent, evidence-forward. No sparkle language in a safety claim.

## Do / Don't examples

| Situation | Don't | Do |
|---|---|---|
| Product headline | "Unlock Your Ultimate Glow-Up Today!" | "Watch it turn pink." |
| CTA button | "Shop Now" | "Take Me There" / "Add to My Pride" |
| Empty cart | "Your cart is empty." | "Nothing here yet. Let's find your Pride." |
| Sold out | "Currently unavailable." | "This one's out roaring somewhere else. Back soon." |
| Safety section | "Formulated with clean ingredients for peace of mind." | "Dermatologist-tested. No parabens. Safe for daily use, ages 8+. Full ingredient list below." |
| Loyalty program | "Earn points with every purchase." | "Every order earns roars. Roars unlock charms." |
| Product description | "This lip oil delivers long-lasting hydration with a glossy finish." | "A tiny drop of shine. Tastes like strawberry. Stars swirl when you shake it." |

## Naming conventions
- Product names: two words max, concrete + sensory. *"Cloud Gloss." "Star Oil." "Mane Mist."*
- Avoid names that only make sense to an adult (no puns on wine, coffee, adult pop culture).
- Shade/variant names: playful and ownable, never generic ("Ballet Pink" → **"First Roar"**; "Clear" → **"Cloud"**).

## Where the voice flexes
- **Instagram captions:** most playful, can use 1 emoji-equivalent (sparkle ✨ only, used sparingly), first person from the girl's POV sometimes.
- **Product pages:** playful headline, competent body copy underneath.
- **Emails to parents:** warmest but most plainly written — this is a trust channel, not a hype channel.
- **Packaging copy:** shortest of all — a headline and one sentence, nothing more. The object does the talking.
